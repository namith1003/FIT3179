Over the past 50 years, global inflation has experienced significant shifts. Beginning with stability in the 1970s, we witnessed high inflation in the 1980s. Subsequently, the 1990s and 2000s brought more consistent prices. This visualization dissects inflation into four key categories: food, energy, consumer, and producer inflation, revealing their influence on the overall trend.

Numbers play a critical role. In 1970, inflation was 4.5%, spiking to 13.6% in the early 1980s. Recent years have seen inflation averaging around 2%. Delve into these subcategories' behaviors, grasp their trends, and understand their impact on global inflation over five decades.
