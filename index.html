Over the past 50 years, the world has witnessed significant fluctuations in inflation rates. Starting with relatively stable prices in the 1970s, we experienced a period of high inflation in the 1980s, followed by more stable inflation in the 1990s and 2000s. This visualization will help you explore the fascinating journey of inflation, not only in its overall context but also dissected into four critical subcategories: food inflation, energy inflation, consumer inflation, and producer inflation. By examining how each of these subcategories contributes to overall inflation, you'll gain insights into the complex interplay of economic factors that have shaped global inflation trends.

Numbers play a pivotal role in this exploration. In 1970, overall inflation stood at a modest 4.5%, but the early 1980s witnessed a surge, with the rate reaching an astonishing 13.6%. In recent years, we've seen a return to more moderate inflation, averaging around 2%. Consumer Inflation Impact, 1970-2022

Energy Inflation Impact, 1970-2022
